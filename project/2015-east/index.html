In-vessel Maintenance Robot for Tokamak

2019-03-01

A robot that works inside Tokamak, an experimental device for nuclear fusion research.

Tokamak is the experiment device for controlled nuclear fusion research, which employs magnetic field to confine the high energy plasma in its toroidal vessel for nuclear fusion activities. The first wall structure of the toroidal vessel is directly exposed to high energy plasma during the experiment and may get damaged after several cycles of plasma discharge experiments. This is a severe threat to the research, and it could affect the safety of the whole Tokamak system. Therefore, to keep the high energy plasma research work continuable and safe, it is necessary to conduct regular inspection operation of the toroidal vessel during the experiment intervals.

Based on the existing in-vessel operation solutions, this project developed an arcuate spreading-arm featured manipulator (see the figure attached) for the Experimental Advanced Superconducting Tokamak (the first superconducting Tokamak in China). The manipulator is made up with four main parts, which are the deployment platform, the arcuate spreading big arm, the planar small arm and the end effector. It can be deployed from the narrow entrance window of the toroidal vessel, can cover the large toroidal vessel workspace, and can adapt to the complex geometry constrains of the D-shape section place in toroidal vessel. A manipulator prototype was manufactured and tested in the EAST toroidal vessel mock-up to validate the overall in-vessel operation process.
